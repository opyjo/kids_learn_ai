# Term 7, Lesson 8: Game Showcase + Badge! 🎮✨

## Teacher's Guide

**Course:** Term 7: Game Builder  
**Age Group:** 9-10 years old  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Term:** 7 of 8 | **Week:** 8 of 8

---

## 📋 Lesson Overview

### Purpose

This is the showcase and celebration week. Students present their Number Guessing Games, play classmates' games, celebrate achievements, and earn their "Game Developer" badge. This is a celebration of learning and a chance to reflect on the term.

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

1. Present their game confidently
2. Appreciate classmates' work
3. Reflect on their learning journey
4. Celebrate their achievements
5. Earn recognition for their hard work

### Materials Needed

- Computer with internet access (for each student)
- Projector/screen share for presentations
- Trinket accounts
- Badges or certificates (Game Developer)
- Optional: Camera for photos
- Optional: Snacks for celebration

### Pre-Lesson Preparation

1. **Review student projects** — Know what each student created
2. **Prepare badges/certificates** — Have Game Developer badges ready
3. **Plan presentation order** — Decide who presents when
4. **Prepare reflection questions** — Have questions ready
5. **Set up celebration** — Make it special!

## 📚 Detailed Teaching Guide

### Part 1: Welcome & Setup (5 minutes)

#### Goals

- Welcome students
- Set expectations
- Build excitement

#### Script/Talking Points

> "Welcome to the Game Showcase! 🎉 Today is all about YOU! You've worked so hard, and now it's time to show off what you've created!"

**Set Expectations:**

> "Here's what we'll do:"
- Each of you will present your game (2-3 minutes)
- Show your welcome screen, gameplay, and features
- Then we'll have a game tournament
- Finally, we'll award Game Developer badges!

**Presentation Tips:**

> "When you present:"
- Speak clearly
- Be proud
- Show at least one complete round
- Share what you learned

**Create Safe Space:**

> "Remember: This is a celebration! We're here to support each other!"

#### Teaching Tips

- Be enthusiastic
- Set positive tone
- Make expectations clear

---

### Part 2: Presentations (40 minutes)

#### Goals

- Each student presents
- Celebrate achievements
- Build confidence

#### Structure

**For Each Student (2-3 minutes):**

1. **Introduction** (30 seconds)
2. **Demonstration** (1-2 minutes)
3. **Reflection** (30 seconds)
4. **Questions** (30 seconds)

#### Teacher Role

- Facilitate
- Support
- Celebrate
- Encourage
- Guide questions

#### Teaching Tips

- Keep energy positive
- Help students who are nervous
- Keep time
- Encourage questions

---

### Part 3: Game Tournament (10 minutes)

#### Goals

- Students play all games
- Build community
- Give feedback

#### Structure

> "Now it's tournament time! Play everyone's games, try to beat high scores, and give compliments!"

**Guidelines:**

- Be respectful
- Be positive
- Give specific compliments
- Have fun!

#### Teacher Role

- Facilitate
- Encourage
- Observe
- Celebrate

#### Teaching Tips

- Let students explore
- Encourage positive interactions
- Take photos
- Enjoy the energy

---

### Part 4: Badge Ceremony (3 minutes)

#### Goals

- Award badges
- Recognize achievements
- Celebrate

#### Script

> "Now for the moment you've been waiting for... the Game Developer Badge ceremony! 🎖️"

**Award Badges:**

Call each student up and award badge.

**Group Celebration:**

> "Let's give a round of applause for all our Game Developers! 🎉"

#### Teaching Tips

- Make it special
- Take photos
- Acknowledge achievements

---

### Part 5: Reflection & Wrap-up (2 minutes)

#### Goals

- Reflect on term
- Celebrate
- Look forward

#### Reflection Questions

> "Let's think about what we learned:"
- What was your favorite thing?
- What are you most proud of?
- What was challenging?
- What's next?

**Final Words:**

> "You've accomplished SO much! Be proud! Keep coding, keep creating, keep being amazing!"

#### Teaching Tips

- Keep it brief but meaningful
- End on high note
- Celebrate the journey

## 🎯 Assessment & Differentiation

### Formative Assessment

**During class, observe:**

- [ ] Can student present their game?
- [ ] Does student understand what they created?
- [ ] Is student proud of their work?
- [ ] Can student answer questions?
- [ ] Does student appreciate others' work?

### Showcase Assessment

This is primarily a celebration, but note:

| Criteria              | Observation Notes             |
| --------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| **Project completion** | Did they finish?             |
| **Presentation**      | How did they present?         |
| **Understanding**     | Do they understand their code? |
| **Creativity**        | Unique features?              |
| **Engagement**        | Participation in tournament?  |

### Differentiation Strategies

#### For Students Who Need More Support

- **Shorter presentation** — 1-2 minutes
- **Support during presentation** — Help if stuck
- **Encourage** — Build confidence
- **Celebrate effort** — Not just completion

#### For Advanced Students

- **Longer presentation** — Can share more
- **Help others** — Assist during tournament
- **Share insights** — What they learned
- **Leadership** — Help facilitate

## ⚠️ Common Challenges & Solutions

### Presentation Anxiety

| Problem                  | Signs                          | Solution                                    |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------- |
| Nervous about presenting | Reluctant, quiet               | Reassure, offer support, shorter time       |
| Forgets what to say      | Stuck                          | Provide simple script, help start          |
| Technical issues         | Program doesn't work           | Help fix quickly, or have backup plan       |

### Time Management

| Problem                  | Signs                          | Solution                                    |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------- |
| Presentations too long   | Going over time                | Gently keep time                            |
| Not enough time          | Rushing                        | Adjust as needed                            |
