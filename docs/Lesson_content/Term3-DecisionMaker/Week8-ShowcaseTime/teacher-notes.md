# Term 3, Lesson 8: Showcase Time! 🎉

## Teacher's Guide

**Course:** Term 3: Decision Maker  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Term:** 3 of 8 | **Week:** 8 of 8

---

## 📋 Lesson Overview

### Purpose

This is a celebration and showcase week! Students present their adventure games, celebrate their achievements, and reflect on their learning journey. This builds confidence, creates closure, and sets up excitement for Term 4.

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will have:

1. Presented their adventure game to the class
2. Celebrated their achievements
3. Reflected on their learning journey
4. Gotten excited for Term 4

### Materials Needed

- Computer with internet access (for presentations)
- Projector/screen share for showcasing
- Camera (optional, for photos)
- Celebration decorations (optional)
- Snacks (optional, check allergies!)

### Pre-Lesson Preparation

1. **Test all adventures** — Make sure they're ready to present
2. **Plan presentation order** — Who goes when?
3. **Prepare celebration activities** — Reflection, sharing, etc.
4. **Preview Term 4** — Get ready to build excitement!

## 📚 Detailed Teaching Guide

### Part 1: Welcome & Celebration Setup (5 minutes)

#### Goals

- Set a celebratory tone
- Build excitement
- Explain the format

#### Script/Talking Points

> "Welcome to the TERM 3 SHOWCASE PARTY! 🎉 Today is all about CELEBRATION! You've worked SO hard this term, learned SO much, and built AMAZING adventure games! Today, you get to show them off, celebrate with your classmates, and reflect on your journey! Let's make this special!"

**Explain the Format:**
- "Each of you will present your adventure game"
- "We'll celebrate each one"
- "Then we'll reflect on what we learned"
- "Finally, we'll get excited for Term 4!"

#### Teaching Tips

- Make it feel like a celebration
- Build excitement
- Emphasize: "This is YOUR day!"

---

### Part 2: Adventure Presentations (30 minutes)

#### Goals

- Each student presents their adventure
- Celebrate each project
- Build confidence
- Learn from each other

#### Presentation Format

For each student (2-3 minutes each):

1. **Introduction**
   - "Hi! My name is [name], and this is my adventure game!"

2. **Demonstration**
   - Run the adventure with some choices
   - Show different paths
   - Show different endings

3. **Highlights**
   - "I'm really proud of [feature]"
   - "I added [personal touch]"

4. **Questions** (optional)
   - Classmates can ask questions
   - Keep it positive and supportive!

#### Teacher's Role

- **Celebrate each presentation** — "Wow! That's amazing!"
- **Point out strengths** — "I love how you [feature]!"
- **Keep it positive** — Focus on what's great
- **Manage time** — Keep presentations moving
- **Take notes** — For reflection later

#### Presentation Tips for Students

Before presentations, remind students:
- Speak clearly
- Show your adventure running
- Be proud of your work!
- It's okay to be nervous—everyone is!

#### Common Scenarios

**If an adventure has bugs:**
- "Let's celebrate what works!"
- "Bugs are normal—you learned from them!"
- Help fix quickly if possible

**If a student is nervous:**
- "You've got this!"
- "We're all here to celebrate with you!"
- Offer to help present if needed

**If presentations run long:**
- Keep to time limits
- Can do "quick showcase" for some
- Focus on celebration, not perfection

---

### Part 3: Reflection & Sharing (15 minutes)

#### Goals

- Reflect on learning journey
- Share favorite moments
- Build community
- Preview what's next

#### Reflection Questions

Ask students to share (voluntarily):

1. **What was your favorite thing to learn?**
   - If statements?
   - Building games?
   - Something else?

2. **What are you most proud of?**
   - Your adventure game?
   - Learning a difficult concept?
   - Helping a classmate?
   - Something else?

3. **What was the hardest part?**
   - Normalize challenges
   - Celebrate overcoming them

4. **What are you excited to learn next?**
   - Build excitement for Term 4!

#### Teaching Tips

- Keep it positive
- Celebrate challenges overcome
- Build excitement for next term
- Make it conversational, not forced

---

### Part 4: Preview Term 4 (5 minutes)

#### Goals

- Build excitement for Term 4
- Preview what's coming
- End on a high note

#### Preview Script

> "Term 3 is complete, but your journey is just beginning! In Term 4: More Choices, you'll learn even MORE ways to make decisions! You'll learn elif (else if) for multiple options, and how to combine conditions with 'and' and 'or'. You'll build bigger games with more choices and paths! It's going to be SO exciting!"

**What's Coming:**
- `elif` statements (multiple options)
- Combining conditions with `and` and `or`
- Building bigger, more complex games
- Creating quiz games

#### Teaching Tips

- Build excitement
- Connect to what they've learned
- End on a high note
- "You're ready for Term 4!"

---

### Part 5: Final Celebration (5 minutes)

#### Goals

- Final congratulations
- Group celebration
- Photos if desired
- End on a high note

#### Final Words

> "Congratulations, Decision Makers! You've completed Term 3 and built amazing adventure games! You should be SO proud of yourselves! You've learned so much and created something amazing! I can't wait to see what you do in Term 4! Let's celebrate!"

#### Teaching Tips

- Make it feel special
- Take group photos
- Celebrate together
- End on a high note
