# Term 4, Lesson 8: Showcase + Badge! 🎉

## Teacher's Guide

**Course:** Term 4: More Choices  
**Age Group:** 9-10 years old  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Term:** 4 of 8 | **Week:** 8 of 8

---

## 📋 Lesson Overview

### Purpose

This is the celebration and showcase lesson where students present their term projects, play each other's games, and earn their "Decision Master" badge. This is a celebration of achievement, a chance to share work, and recognition of all the learning that happened this term.

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will have:

1. Presented their adventure games to the class
2. Played and enjoyed other students' adventures
3. Earned their "Decision Master" badge
4. Celebrated their achievements and growth

### Materials Needed

- Computer with internet access (for each student)
- Projector/screen share for presentations
- Trinket accounts
- Decision Master badges (physical or digital)
- Camera for photos (optional)
- Celebration snacks (optional)
- Certificate templates (optional)

### Pre-Lesson Preparation

1. **Review student projects** — Know what each student built
2. **Prepare badges** — Physical or digital badges ready
3. **Plan presentation order** — Who presents when
4. **Prepare celebration** — Snacks, music, decorations (optional)
5. **Prepare certificates** — If using certificates
6. **Test all projects** — Make sure they run

## 📚 Detailed Teaching Guide

### Part 1: Welcome & Introduction (5 minutes)

#### Goals

- Set celebratory tone
- Explain the day's activities
- Build excitement

#### The Welcome

> "Welcome to SHOWCASE DAY! 🎉 This is YOUR day to shine! You've worked so hard this term, and today we're going to celebrate everything you've accomplished!"

**Explain the day:**
1. **Presentations** — Each student shares their adventure
2. **Playing Games** — Try each other's adventures
3. **Badge Ceremony** — Earn your Decision Master badge!

**Set expectations:**
- Be supportive of each other
- Ask questions
- Celebrate everyone's work
- Have fun!

#### Teaching Tips

- Be enthusiastic and celebratory
- Set positive, supportive tone
- Make it feel special
- Keep it brief—get to presentations

---

### Part 2: Presentations (30 minutes)

#### Goals

- Each student presents their adventure
- Celebrate individual achievements
- Build confidence

#### Presentation Structure

**For each student (2-3 minutes):**

1. **Introduction** — Name and adventure theme
2. **Demo** — Run the adventure, show one path
3. **Highlights** — Favorite part, what they're proud of
4. **Questions** — Answer 1-2 questions
5. **Applause** — Celebrate!

**Presentation prompts:**
- "Tell us about your adventure!"
- "What's your favorite part?"
- "What are you most proud of?"
- "What was the hardest part?"

**Keep it positive:**
- Celebrate what they built
- Focus on achievements
- Be supportive
- Keep it brief

#### Teaching Tips

- Keep presentations short (2-3 min each)
- Help shy students
- Celebrate each presentation
- Keep energy positive
- Take notes for badges

---

### Part 3: Playing Games (20 minutes)

#### Goals

- Students play each other's games
- Try different paths
- Give positive feedback
- Have fun!

#### Playing Time

> "Now let's play each other's games! Try at least 3 different adventures, explore different paths, and see all the creativity!"

**Structure:**
- Students can move around
- Try different adventures
- Share Trinket links
- Give positive feedback
- Have fun!

**Encourage:**
- Try different paths
- See all endings
- Notice creativity
- Give compliments

#### Teaching Tips

- Let them explore freely
- Be available for help
- Encourage trying different games
- Keep it fun and positive
- Take photos if possible

---

### Part 4: Badge Ceremony (5 minutes)

#### Goals

- Award Decision Master badges
- Recognize achievements
- Make it special

#### The Ceremony

> "You've all earned your Decision Master badge! 🏆 You learned if, else, elif, and, or, built menus and quizzes, and created amazing adventure games! You're Decision Masters!"

**Award badges:**
- Call each student up
- Present badge
- Say something specific about their achievement
- Take photo (optional)
- Applause!

**What they earned:**
- ✅ Completed Term 4
- ✅ Built expanded adventure game
- ✅ Used elif for multiple choices
- ✅ Created 4+ endings
- ✅ Added scoring/inventory
- ✅ Presented their work

#### Teaching Tips

- Make it special and personal
- Say something specific about each student
- Take photos if possible
- Celebrate each one
- Make it memorable

---

## 🎯 Assessment & Recognition

### Badge Requirements

Students earn the Decision Master badge by:

- ✅ Completing Term 4
- ✅ Building expanded adventure game
- ✅ Using elif correctly
- ✅ Creating 4+ endings
- ✅ Adding scoring/inventory
- ✅ Presenting their work

### Recognition Ideas

- **Badges** — Physical or digital
- **Certificates** — Print certificates
- **Photos** — Take photos with badges
- **Shout-outs** — Recognize specific achievements
- **Display** — Show projects on screen

## 📊 Post-Lesson Reflection

### After the Showcase, Note:

1. **What went well?**
   - Did students present well?
   - Were they proud of their work?
   - Did they enjoy playing each other's games?

2. **Highlights:**
   - Most creative adventure
   - Best presentation
   - Most complex game
   - Best use of elif
   - Most creative endings

3. **Individual achievements:**
   - Who exceeded expectations?
   - Who showed great growth?
   - Who needs encouragement for Term 5?

4. **Term 4 summary:**
   - Overall success
   - Key learnings
   - Areas for improvement
   - Preparation for Term 5
