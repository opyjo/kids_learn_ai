# Year 2, Lesson 7: Portfolio & Presentation Prep 📁🎤

## Teacher's Guide

**Course:** Capstone & Portfolio (Year 2, Term 8)
**Duration:** 60-75 minutes
**Format:** Live online via Zoom
**Term:** 8 of 8 | **Week:** 7 of 8

---

## 📋 Lesson Overview

### Purpose

The penultimate lesson. Students assemble a portfolio of all eight Year 2 projects and rehearse their graduation presentation. This is where a year of work becomes a body of work they can show anyone. Goals:

1. **Assemble the portfolio** — gather all 8 project Trinket links with titles and one-line descriptions, built as a "Portfolio Index" program
2. **Make everything shareable** — check every Trinket's sharing is on (dead links are the #1 portfolio failure)
3. **Rehearse the demo** — practise the 5-step capstone demo for graduation
4. **Build presentation confidence** — short, live, proud, calm under a live bug

### Learning Objectives

Students will be able to:
1. Build a Portfolio Index program (list of dicts → f-string loop) listing their 8 projects
2. Verify each project's Trinket link is shareable
3. Deliver the 5-step demo (introduce → run → show → explain one proud feature → take a question)
4. Give a short, confident presentation

### Key Success Metrics
- [ ] Every student has a Portfolio Index listing all 8 projects
- [ ] Every Trinket link is confirmed shareable
- [ ] Every student rehearses their capstone demo at least once
- [ ] Students feel ready (not nervous) for graduation

### Materials Needed
- Zoom with screen share and breakout rooms (rehearsal pairs)
- Students' 8 Year 2 project Trinkets + the capstone
- This guide; class WhatsApp/email

### Pre-Lesson Preparation
1. Remind students in advance to locate all 8 of their Year 2 projects.
2. Have the list of the 8 Year 2 projects ready (Smart Calculator, Arcade, Text Adventure, Contact Manager, AI Investigation, AI Assistant, Data Story, Capstone).
3. Pre-build the Portfolio Index example and run it.
4. Prepare rehearsal pairs.


## 📚 Detailed Teaching Guide

### Part 1: What Is a Portfolio? (6 min)
> "Real developers don't just say 'I can code' — they SHOW their work. A portfolio is your collection of proof. By the end of today, you'll have one you could show a teacher, a parent, or one day a school or job."

### Part 2: Your Eight Year 2 Projects (10 min)
Walk the eight projects together as a journey — from the Smart Calculator to the Capstone. Let students feel the scale of what they built.

### Part 3: Trinket Check — Make Everything Shareable (10 min)
The critical practical step: every Trinket must be shared/public or the link is dead. Walk through checking the share setting.
- **Teaching tip:** dead links are the most common portfolio failure — budget real time here and verify with a partner.

### Part 4: Build the Portfolio Index (19 min)
Build the Portfolio Index program together: a list of dicts (project name, description, link) printed with an f-string loop and a nice banner. This is itself a portfolio artefact and a neat callback to Term 4/7 skills.
- **Teaching tip:** students paste their real links into the dicts as they go.

### Part 5: The 5-Step Demo + Rehearsal (17 min)
Teach the 5-step demo (introduce → run live → show it working → explain one proud feature → take a question). Then breakout pairs rehearse their capstone demo and time each other.
- **Teaching tip:** emphasise "short and proud." Remind them a live bug is normal — stay calm, say "let me debug that," move on.

### Wrap-Up
Homework: finish the portfolio (all 8 links + descriptions) and rehearse. Tease Week 8: GRADUATION.

## 🎓 Assessment & Notes

**Observe:**
- [ ] Portfolio Index runs and lists all 8 projects
- [ ] All Trinket links verified shareable
- [ ] Rehearsed the demo at least once
- [ ] Presentation is short, clear, confident

**Need support:** students missing project files (help them locate or rebuild a link); very nervous presenters (offer alternatives — you run their link, or they describe one feature).
**Ready for more:** encourage a "what I'm proudest of" line per project.

---

## ⚠️ Troubleshooting Guide

| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| A Trinket link doesn't open | Sharing is off — walk through the share/public setting |
| Student lost a project | Help locate it in their account, or note it as "rebuilt" — don't let it derail the portfolio |
| Portfolio Index crashes | Check the list-of-dicts syntax and that the loop reads the right keys |
| Demo runs too long | Coach "short and proud" — two minutes, one proud feature |
| Very nervous presenter | Offer: you run their shared link, or they describe one feature verbally |
| Behind schedule | Prioritise shareable links + the index; rehearsal can extend into homework |

---

## 📊 Post-Lesson Reflection
- Does every student have a complete, shareable portfolio? Chase any missing links before graduation.
- Who is demo-ready and who needs a gentle plan for next week?
- Note standout portfolios to celebrate at graduation.

## 💭 Remember

**A portfolio turns a year of lessons into evidence.** For many students this is the first time they see everything they've made laid out together — it's a genuinely proud moment. Make sure every link works, because next week they show the world. Graduation is one lesson away.
