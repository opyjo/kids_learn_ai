# Year 2, Lesson 8: GRADUATION — Portfolio Showcase 🎓🎉

## Teacher's Guide

**Course:** Capstone & Portfolio (Year 2, Term 8)
**Duration:** 60-75 minutes
**Format:** Live online via Zoom
**Term:** 8 of 8 | **Week:** 8 of 8

---

## 📋 Lesson Overview

### Purpose

The final lesson of Year 2 — a graduation ceremony. Students present their capstone and portfolio, receive the **AI-Ready Developer Certificate**, reflect on the whole year, and look ahead. There is almost no new content; today is celebration, recognition, and closure. Goals:

1. **Celebrate every student** — each one demos their capstone and is applauded by name
2. **Honour the whole journey** — from Term 1's Code Sprint to a self-designed capstone across eight projects
3. **Award the certificate** — a genuine milestone moment
4. **Send them onward** — encourage them to keep building beyond the course

This is the emotional high point of the year. Your warmth and pride set the tone. Prepare to make every child feel seen.

### Learning Objectives (celebration-focused)

Students will be able to:
1. Present their capstone confidently (live demo + one proud feature)
2. Show their portfolio of eight Year 2 projects
3. Reflect on how they've grown as a coder
4. Name something they want to build or learn next

### Key Success Metrics
- [ ] Every student presents (or has their work shown) and is celebrated by name
- [ ] Every student receives the AI-Ready Developer Certificate
- [ ] The class leaves proud and excited to keep coding
- [ ] Every student names a "what's next"

### Materials Needed
- Zoom with screen share; celebratory energy
- Students' capstone + portfolio (all 8 projects)
- The AI-Ready Developer Certificate (printed program and/or a real certificate to send afterwards)
- This guide; class WhatsApp/email; optional: invite parents to the last 15 minutes

### Pre-Lesson Preparation
1. Confirm each student's capstone runs and portfolio links work (from Week 7).
2. Prepare the certificate for each student (name ready to call out).
3. Plan the presentation order — volunteers first, gently include quieter students.
4. Have a backup for anyone whose program breaks (present the class solution / describe a feature).
5. Consider inviting parents/guardians to witness the ceremony.

## 🎯 Lesson Flow (60-75 Minutes)

```
⏱️  0-6 min   → Warm welcome — this is the finish line
⏱️  6-12 min  → Final polish minute + line up presentations
⏱️ 12-40 min  → Capstone + portfolio showcase (every student)
⏱️ 40-48 min  → Year-in-review: the whole journey
⏱️ 48-58 min  → The Certificate Ceremony 🎓
⏱️ 58-68 min  → Reflection: you, then and now
⏱️ 68-75 min  → Where your journey goes next + final send-off
```

**Flexible timing:** demo time scales with class size — for a large class, cap demos at ~2 minutes or run two rounds. Never cut the certificate ceremony or the reflection; they are the heart of the day.

## 📚 Detailed Teaching Guide

### Part 1: Warm Welcome (6 min)
> "This is it — the finish line of Year 2. Eight terms ago you were shaking the rust off print(). Today you demo a program YOU designed. I am so proud of every one of you."

### Part 2: Final Polish Minute (6 min)
A quick run-through so every capstone is demo-ready. No new features — just confirm it runs.

### Part 3: The Grand Showcase (28 min)
Every student presents: capstone demo (5-step format from Week 7) + a glance at their portfolio of eight projects. Celebrate each one **by name**; lead applause/🔥 in the chat.
- **Teaching tip:** protect nervous students — offer to run their shared link or let them describe one feature. Everyone presents in some form.
- **Handle live bugs warmly:** "A live bug — every developer's badge of honour!"

### Part 4: Look How Far You Came (8 min)
Show the full Year 2 roadmap, all eight terms ✅ with their projects. Name the arc: calculator → games → adventures → data tools → AI understanding → assistant → data story → their own capstone.

### Part 5: The Certificate Ceremony (10 min)
The big moment. Call each student by name and award the **AI-Ready Developer Certificate**. Optionally run the certificate-printer program live for each name. Make it feel ceremonial.
- **Teaching tip:** if parents are present, this is the moment to invite them in.

### Part 6: Reflection — You, Then and Now (10 min)
Students share: proudest project of the year / how they've changed as a coder. Read several aloud.

### Part 7: Where Your Journey Goes Next (7 min)
Encourage continued building — more projects, a new library, teaching someone else. Frame the end as a beginning: "There's no next class, but your journey doesn't stop here."

### Final Send-Off
A heartfelt close. Stay on the call for goodbyes and photos.

## 🎓 Assessment & Notes

**End-of-year picture (celebration, not grading):**
- Every student completed a capstone and a portfolio — that is the achievement.
- Note each student's growth and a "what's next" suggestion you can share with them/parents.

**Where students can go next:**
- Keep building capstone v2; try a new Trinket type (turtle games); learn a beginner library outside Trinket; teach a sibling/friend; enter a kids' coding challenge.

## ⚠️ Troubleshooting Guide

| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| A capstone won't run live | Present the polished class solution or have the student describe a feature |
| Student too nervous to present | Run their shared link for them; or they narrate one feature |
| Portfolio link is dead | Note it and celebrate the project anyway; fix sharing afterwards |
| Large class, not enough time | Cap demos at ~2 min or two rounds; never cut the ceremony |
| Emotional moment / tears of pride | Lean in — it's earned; give space and warmth |
| Certificate program errors | Have a pre-run output or a real certificate ready as backup |

## 📊 Post-Lesson Reflection
- Did every student feel celebrated and receive their certificate?
- Which growth stories stood out? Share with parents/records.

- Follow up with any student who missed graduation so they still get their moment and certificate.
