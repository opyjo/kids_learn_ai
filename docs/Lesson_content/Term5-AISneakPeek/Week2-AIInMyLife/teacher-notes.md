# Term 5, Lesson 2: AI in Your Life! 🕵️

## Teacher's Guide

**Course:** Term 5: AI Sneak Peek  
**Age Group:** 9-10 years old  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Term:** 5 of 8 | **Week:** 2 of 8

---

## 📋 Lesson Overview

### Purpose

This lesson deepens students' understanding of AI by having them actively identify and categorize AI in their daily lives. Students become "AI detectives" who can spot AI everywhere. This builds awareness of how pervasive AI is and helps them understand different types of AI applications.

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

1. Identify AI in multiple categories (entertainment, games, phones, shopping, maps)
2. Understand what different types of AI do
3. Categorize AI by function or area of life
4. Recognize AI they didn't realize was AI
5. Create a program listing AI they use

### Materials Needed

- Computer with internet access (for each student)
- Projector/screen share for demonstrations
- Whiteboard or chart paper for categorizing
- Optional: Examples of AI apps (YouTube, games, etc.)
- Optional: Stickers or rewards for AI detectives

### Pre-Lesson Preparation

1. **Review AI categories** — Entertainment, games, phones, shopping, maps
2. **Prepare examples** — Have ready examples from each category
3. **Think about student examples** — What AI do they likely use?
4. **Prepare coding activity** — Simple program structure
5. **Plan discussion questions** — "What AI did you discover?"

## 📚 Detailed Teaching Guide

### Part 1: Welcome & Review (5 minutes)

#### Goals

- Quick review of what AI is
- Set context for today's exploration
- Get students excited about being AI detectives

#### The Review

> "Welcome back! Last week, we learned what AI is. Quick review: What is AI?"

Wait for: Computers doing smart things, learning, etc.

> "Perfect! Today, we're going to become AI DETECTIVES! We're going to find ALL the AI in your life. You're going to discover that AI is EVERYWHERE!"

#### Teaching Tips

- Keep review brief
- Build excitement about detective work
- Set the tone for exploration

---

### Part 2: AI Categories (15 minutes)

#### Goals

- Explore AI in different areas of life
- Show examples from each category
- Help students understand different types

#### The Exploration

Go through each category with examples:

**1. Entertainment AI (5 min)**
- YouTube, Netflix, TikTok, Spotify
- How they learn and recommend
- Show examples if possible

**2. Game AI (3 min)**
- Smart characters
- Adaptive difficulty
- Examples from games they know

**3. Phone AI (3 min)**
- Siri, autocorrect, face recognition
- How they help daily

**4. Shopping & Maps AI (4 min)**
- Product recommendations
- Maps and navigation
- How they learn patterns

#### Teaching Tips

- Use examples students actually use
- Keep it interactive—ask what they use
- Show enthusiasm for each category
- Connect to their daily experience

---

### Part 3: AI Detective Activity (15 minutes)

#### Goals

- Students actively identify AI
- Work in groups to find examples
- Build awareness of AI pervasiveness

#### The Activity

> "Now it's YOUR turn! Work in pairs or small groups. Your mission: Find 10+ examples of AI you use! Categorize them too!"

**Give structure:**
- Entertainment: ___
- Games: ___
- Phone: ___
- Shopping: ___
- Other: ___

**Circulate and help:**
- Help groups that are stuck
- Validate all reasonable answers
- Encourage creativity
- Celebrate discoveries

**After 10 minutes, share:**
- Have groups share their findings
- Write examples on board
- Celebrate discoveries

#### Teaching Tips

- Give clear time limits
- Encourage collaboration
- Validate all reasonable answers
- Make it fun and competitive
- Help groups that are stuck

---

### Part 4: Coding Activity (15 minutes)

#### Goals

- Create a program listing AI
- Practice Python skills
- Organize AI discoveries

#### The Program

> "Now let's create a program that lists all the AI you discovered!"

**Show structure:**

```python
print("=== AI IN MY LIFE ===")
print("")
print("ENTERTAINMENT:")
print("1. YouTube - recommends videos")
# ... etc
```

**Guide students:**
- Help them organize by category
- Encourage creativity
- Help with formatting
- Celebrate their programs

#### Teaching Tips

- Keep it simple—focus on organization
- Help with formatting
- Encourage creativity
- Don't worry about complex code

---

### Part 5: Share & Discuss (7 minutes)

#### Goals

- Share discoveries
- Discuss what surprised them
- Build awareness

#### The Discussion

> "What AI did you discover? What surprised you?"

**Discussion questions:**
- What AI did you find that surprised you?
- Which category has the most AI?
- How often do you use AI?
- What would life be like without AI?

#### Teaching Tips

- Encourage sharing
- Validate all discoveries
- Build excitement
- Connect to their lives

---

### Part 6: Wrap-up & Homework (3 minutes)

#### Review Key Points

> "What did we discover today?"

Quick review:
1. AI is everywhere
2. Different categories of AI
3. We use AI all the time

#### Introduce Homework

> "For homework, create a program listing 8+ AI examples you use!"

#### Preview Next Week

> "Next week, we'll explore the difference between smart AI and regular programs!"

## 🤖 AI Activity: AI Diary (5-10 minutes)

### What to Do

1. **Introduction** (1 min)
   > "You use AI every day, but do you notice it? Let's keep track of every time you interact with AI!"

2. **AI Diary Activity** (4-5 min)
   - Have students create a simple "AI Diary" - a list where they write down every AI interaction
   - Examples: "Used Siri", "Watched YouTube recommendations", "Played a game with AI"
   - Share: "Who interacted with AI the most today?"
   - Discuss: "Were you surprised how often you use AI?"

3. **Discussion** (2-3 min)
   - "How many times did you interact with AI today?"
   - "What AI did you use that you didn't realize was AI?"
   - "Why do you think AI is so common?"

4. **Connection** (1 min)
   > "By tracking your AI interactions, you're becoming AI-aware - noticing AI everywhere in your life! This helps you understand how much AI is part of your daily routine!"

### Discussion Questions

- "How many times did you interact with AI today?"
- "What AI did you use that you didn't realize was AI?"
- "Why do you think AI is so common?"

### Teaching Tips

- Make it a challenge - "Who can find the most AI interactions?"
- Encourage students to track for homework too
- Use examples: "Did you use voice search? That's AI!"
- Emphasize AI awareness: "You're becoming AI-aware!"
- Connect to their learning: "You're learning how all this AI works!"

### Alternative Activity (If Short on Time)

- Quick discussion: "Who can name 5 times they used AI today?"
- Connect: "AI is everywhere - you just learned to notice it!"
- Emphasize: "Being AI-aware helps you understand how AI works!"

## 🎯 Assessment & Differentiation

### Formative Assessment

**During class, observe:**
- [ ] Can student identify AI examples?
- [ ] Does student understand different categories?
- [ ] Can student categorize AI?
- [ ] Is student engaged in detective work?

### Homework Assessment: "My AI Discovery Program"

| Criteria | Exceeds (3) | Meets (2) | Developing (1) |
|----------|-------------|-----------|----------------|
| **Number of examples** | 10+ examples | 8 examples | Fewer than 8 |
| **Categorization** | Well-organized categories | Some categories | No categories |
| **Understanding** | Clear explanations | Some explanations | Little explanation |
| **Code quality** | Well-formatted, creative | Functional | Basic or errors |
| **Comments** | Helpful comments | Some comments | No comments |

### Differentiation Strategies

#### For Students Who Need More Support

- Provide a template with categories
- Give examples to choose from
- Reduce requirement to 5-6 examples
- Pair with a peer
- Focus on familiar AI

#### For Advanced Students

- Challenge with less obvious AI
- Ask them to explain HOW each AI works
- Encourage research into one AI
- Challenge with 15+ examples
- Ask them to help peers

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls & Solutions

### Conceptual Issues

| Problem | Prevention | Solution |
|---------|------------|----------|
| Can't identify AI | Provide many examples first | Give hints, use familiar examples |
| Confused about categories | Show clear examples | Use visual organization |
| Overwhelmed by options | Structure the activity | Give categories to fill in |
