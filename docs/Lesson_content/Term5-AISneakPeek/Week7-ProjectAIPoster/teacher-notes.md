# Term 5, Lesson 7: Project - AI in My Life Poster! 🎨

## Teacher's Guide

**Course:** Term 5: AI Sneak Peek  
**Age Group:** 9-10 years old  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Term:** 5 of 8 | **Week:** 7 of 8

---

## 📋 Lesson Overview

### Purpose

This is the term project lesson where students create an "AI in My Life" poster showing all the AI they use or encounter. This project synthesizes all AI concepts learned this term while allowing creative expression. Students can create a Python program, digital poster, or hand-drawn poster.

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will have:

1. Planned their AI in My Life poster
2. Started creating their poster
3. Included 5+ examples of AI they use
4. Explained what each AI does
5. Created something creative and personal

### Materials Needed

- Computer with internet access (for each student)
- Projector/screen share for demonstrations
- Trinket accounts (for Python option)
- Optional: Canva, Google Slides access (for digital option)
- Optional: Art supplies (for hand-drawn option)
- Optional: Camera/phone (to photograph hand-drawn posters)

### Pre-Lesson Preparation

1. **Review project requirements** — Ensure you understand all requirements
2. **Prepare format examples** — Show examples of each format option
3. **Think about AI examples** — Help students brainstorm
4. **Prepare tools** — Canva, Google Slides, etc. if using
5. **Plan support strategy** — How to help students choose format

## 📚 Detailed Teaching Guide

### Part 1: Introduction (5 minutes)

#### Goals

- Set context for the project
- Review requirements clearly
- Get students excited

#### The Introduction

> "This is your TERM 5 PROJECT! You've learned so much about AI this term. Now you're going to create a poster showing all the AI in your life!"

**Review requirements:**
- 5+ examples of AI
- What each AI does
- Is it helpful? (your opinion)
- One question about AI
- Creative and fun!

**Show examples if available**

#### Teaching Tips

- Build excitement
- Make requirements clear
- Show examples if possible
- Keep it brief—get them creating

---

### Part 2: Format Options (10 minutes)

#### Goals

- Show all three format options
- Help students choose
- Set up for success

#### The Options

**Option 1: Python Program**
- Show example
- Good for students who like coding
- Easy to share via Trinket

**Option 2: Digital Poster**
- Show example (Canva, Slides, etc.)
- Good for students who like design
- Visual and creative

**Option 3: Hand-Drawn**
- Show example
- Good for students who like drawing
- Very personal

**Help students choose:**
> "Which format sounds most fun to you? Choose what you're most excited about!"

#### Teaching Tips

- Show examples of each
- Help students choose
- Don't force a format
- Support their choice

---

### Part 3: Planning (10 minutes)

#### Goals

- Students plan their posters
- List AI examples
- Think about layout

#### The Planning

> "Before you create, let's plan! List 5+ AI examples you want to include."

**Help students:**
- Brainstorm AI examples
- Think about what each does
- Consider their opinions
- Think of a question

**Give them 7-8 minutes to plan**

**Circulate and help:**
- Help brainstorm
- Guide structure
- Ensure they have enough examples

#### Teaching Tips

- Don't skip planning
- Help those stuck
- Encourage creativity
- Check they have enough examples

---

### Part 4: Creating (30 minutes)

#### Goals

- Students create their posters
- Apply AI learning
- Create something they're proud of

#### The Creation

> "Now it's time to create! Start building your poster. Make it creative, make it fun, make it yours!"

**Circulate and support:**
- Help with tools (Canva, etc.)
- Help with code (Python option)
- Encourage creativity
- Help with structure
- Celebrate progress

**Common support needed:**
- Formatting help
- Tool help (Canva, etc.)
- Code help (Python)
- Brainstorming help

#### Teaching Tips

- Give them space to create
- Help when stuck
- Encourage creativity
- Don't hover
- Celebrate progress

---

### Part 5: Wrap-up (3 minutes)

#### Goals

- Preview next week
- Build excitement
- Remind about finishing

#### The Wrap-up

> "What amazing posters you're creating! Next week, we'll SHOWCASE them! You'll present your posters, share your AI discoveries, and earn your 'AI Curious' badge!"

**Homework reminder:**
> "Finish polishing your poster for next week's showcase!"

**Build excitement:**
> "I can't wait to see everyone's posters next week!"

## 🎯 Assessment & Differentiation

### Formative Assessment

**During class, observe:**
- [ ] Does student have 5+ AI examples?
- [ ] Does student understand requirements?
- [ ] Is student engaged in creating?
- [ ] Is poster progressing well?

### Project Assessment: "AI in My Life Poster"

| Criteria | Exceeds (3) | Meets (2) | Developing (1) |
|----------|-------------|-----------|----------------|
| **Number of examples** | 7+ AI examples | 5 examples | Fewer than 5 |
| **Explanations** | Clear, detailed explanations | Adequate explanations | Basic explanations |
| **Helpful opinions** | Thoughtful opinions for each | Some opinions | Missing opinions |
| **Question about AI** | Thoughtful question | One question | Missing question |
| **Creativity** | Highly creative, engaging | Creative elements | Basic |
| **Presentation ready** | Polished, ready to present | Mostly ready | Needs work |

### Differentiation Strategies

#### For Students Who Need More Support

- Provide a template
- Help brainstorm AI examples
- Reduce to 5 examples (not 7+)
- Provide sentence starters
- Pair with a peer
- Focus on getting it done

#### For Advanced Students

- Challenge with 10+ examples
- Encourage deeper explanations
- Multiple questions about AI
- More creative formats
- Ask them to help peers
- Encourage research into one AI

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls & Solutions

### Technical Issues

| Problem | Prevention | Solution |
|---------|------------|----------|
| Can't choose format | Show clear examples | Help them decide based on interests |
| Struggles with tools | Provide tool help | Show how to use Canva, etc. |
| Can't think of examples | Provide list to choose from | Help brainstorm |

### Conceptual Issues

| Problem | Signs | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Doesn't understand requirements | Missing elements | Review requirements clearly |
| Can't explain what AI does | Vague explanations | Help them think through each AI |
| No opinion on helpfulness | Missing opinions | Ask: "Does this help you? How?" |
