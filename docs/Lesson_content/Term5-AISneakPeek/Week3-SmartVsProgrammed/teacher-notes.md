# Term 5, Lesson 3: Smart vs Programmed! 🧠

## Teacher's Guide

**Course:** Term 5: AI Sneak Peek  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Term:** 5 of 8 | **Week:** 3 of 8

---

## 📋 Lesson Overview

### Purpose

This lesson helps students understand the fundamental difference between regular programs (what they've been making) and AI programs (smart programs that learn). This is a crucial conceptual lesson that helps students appreciate what they've learned while understanding how AI builds on those same skills.

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

1. Distinguish between regular programs and AI programs
2. Identify characteristics of each type
3. Understand when to use regular vs AI programs
4. See how AI builds on regular programming skills
5. Appreciate the value of both types

### Materials Needed

- Computer with internet access (for each student)
- Projector/screen share for demonstrations
- Whiteboard for comparison chart
- Examples of regular programs (calculator, simple game)
- Examples of AI programs (YouTube, Siri demo)

### Pre-Lesson Preparation

1. **Review concepts** — Regular vs AI programs
2. **Prepare examples** — Have clear examples of each
3. **Prepare comparison chart** — Visual aid showing differences
4. **Think about student programs** — Examples from their work
5. **Plan discussion questions** — "Is this smart or programmed?"

## 📚 Detailed Teaching Guide

### Part 1: Welcome & Hook (5 minutes)

#### Goals

- Set up the big question
- Get students thinking
- Create curiosity

#### The Hook

> "You've been making programs. You've learned about AI. But here's a BIG question: What's the DIFFERENCE? How is a regular program different from an AI program? Let's find out!"

**Ask the question:**
> "Think about your grade calculator. Is it 'smart' like YouTube, or is it different? Let's explore!"

#### Teaching Tips

- Build curiosity
- Don't answer yet—let them think
- Make it feel like a discovery

---

### Part 2: Regular Programs (15 minutes)

#### Goals

- Understand what regular programs are
- See examples from their work
- Recognize characteristics

#### The Exploration

**Show their grade calculator:**
```python
score = 85
if score >= 80:
    print("You passed!")
```

> "This is a REGULAR program. What does it do?"

**Discuss characteristics:**
- Follows exact instructions
- Does the same thing every time
- Predictable
- You control every step

**More examples:**
- Calculator
- Simple games
- Their adventure games

#### Teaching Tips

- Use their actual programs
- Make them feel accomplished
- Emphasize that regular programs are valuable
- Keep it positive

---

### Part 3: AI Programs (15 minutes)

#### Goals

- Understand what AI programs are
- See how they're different
- Recognize characteristics

#### The Exploration

**Show YouTube example:**
> "YouTube is an AI program. How is it different?"

**Discuss characteristics:**
- Learns from examples
- Adapts and changes
- Recognizes patterns
- Gets better over time

**More examples:**
- Siri/Alexa
- Smart game characters
- Recommendations

#### Teaching Tips

- Use familiar examples
- Emphasize the "learning" aspect
- Show how it adapts
- Make it clear but not overwhelming

---

### Part 4: Comparison (10 minutes)

#### Goals

- See differences side-by-side
- Understand when to use each
- See how they connect

#### The Comparison

**Create a chart on board:**

| Regular | AI |
|--------|-----|
| Exact rules | Learns from examples |
| Same every time | Adapts |
| Predictable | Gets smarter |

**Discuss:**
- When to use regular programs
- When to use AI programs
- How they connect

#### Teaching Tips

- Use visual chart
- Make comparison clear
- Emphasize both are valuable
- Show the connection

---

### Part 5: Practice Activity (10 minutes)

#### Goals

- Practice identifying types
- Apply understanding
- Build confidence

#### The Activity

> "Let's practice! Is each example 'smart' (AI) or 'programmed' (regular)?"

**Go through examples:**
- Calculator → Programmed
- YouTube → Smart
- Simple game → Programmed
- Siri → Smart
- Their programs → Programmed

**Have students explain why**

#### Teaching Tips

- Make it interactive
- Validate correct answers
- Help with reasoning
- Celebrate understanding

---

### Part 6: Wrap-up & Homework (3 minutes)

#### Review Key Points

> "What did we learn today?"

Quick review:
1. Regular programs: Exact rules, predictable
2. AI programs: Learn, adapt, get smarter
3. Both are important!
4. AI builds on regular programming skills

#### Introduce Homework

> "For homework, create a program showing the difference between regular and AI!"

#### Preview Next Week

> "Next week, we'll explore HOW AI learns!"

## 🎯 Assessment & Differentiation

### Formative Assessment

**During class, observe:**
- [ ] Can student distinguish regular vs AI?
- [ ] Does student understand characteristics?
- [ ] Can student identify examples?
- [ ] Does student see the connection?

### Homework Assessment: "Smart vs Programmed Comparison"

| Criteria | Exceeds (3) | Meets (2) | Developing (1) |
|----------|-------------|-----------|----------------|
| **Understanding** | Clear explanation of difference | Some understanding | Confused about difference |
| **Examples** | Good examples of both | Some examples | Few examples |
| **Code quality** | Well-formatted, clear | Functional | Basic or errors |
| **Comments** | Helpful explanations | Some comments | No comments |

### Differentiation Strategies

#### For Students Who Need More Support

- Provide clear examples
- Use visual aids
- Focus on one type at a time
- Pair with a peer
- Simplify the comparison

#### For Advanced Students

- Challenge with complex examples
- Ask them to explain HOW AI learns
- Encourage creating examples
- Ask them to help peers
- Challenge with "make it smart" exercises

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls & Solutions

### Conceptual Issues

| Problem | Prevention | Solution |
|---------|------------|----------|
| Thinks regular programs are "bad" | Emphasize both are valuable | "Both are important!" |
| Confused about difference | Use clear examples | Show side-by-side comparison |
| Can't identify types | Provide many examples | Practice with familiar examples |
