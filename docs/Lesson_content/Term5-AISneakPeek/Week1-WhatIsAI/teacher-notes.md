# Term 5, Lesson 1: What IS AI? 🤖

## Teacher's Guide

**Course:** Term 5: AI Sneak Peek  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Term:** 5 of 8 | **Week:** 1 of 8

---

## 📋 Lesson Overview

### Purpose

This lesson introduces students to Artificial Intelligence in an age-appropriate, engaging way. Term 5 is designed as a "breather term" that steps back from heavy coding to explore AI concepts and connect everything students have learned to real-world AI applications. This first lesson sets the foundation by defining AI, busting myths, and showing students that AI is everywhere in their daily lives.

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

1. Define Artificial Intelligence in simple terms
2. Identify AI in their daily lives (Siri, YouTube, games, etc.)
3. Distinguish between regular programs and AI programs
4. Understand how their Python skills connect to AI
5. Feel excited and curious about AI (not scared!)

### Materials Needed

- Computer with internet access (for teacher demonstration)
- Projector/screen share for presentations
- Whiteboard or chart paper for brainstorming
- Optional: Examples of AI (phone with Siri, tablet with YouTube, etc.)
- Optional: Stickers or small rewards for participation
- Optional: Printed AI examples handout

### Pre-Lesson Preparation

1. **Review AI concepts** — Refresh your understanding of AI basics
2. **Prepare examples** — Have ready: YouTube, Siri demo, game examples
3. **Prepare myths discussion** — Be ready to address common fears
4. **Test technology** — Ensure Siri/Alexa demo will work (if using)
5. **Send welcome message** — Get students excited about Term 5!

## 📚 Detailed Teaching Guide

### Part 1: Welcome to Term 5! (5 minutes)

#### Goals

- Celebrate students' progress (4 terms completed!)
- Introduce Term 5 as a special "AI exploration" term
- Get students excited about AI
- Set expectations (lighter coding, more concepts)

#### Script/Talking Points

> "Welcome back to KidsLearnAI! I'm SO excited to see you all! You've completed FOUR TERMS—that's incredible! Give yourselves a huge round of applause!"

**Introduce Term 5:**

> "Term 5 is going to be DIFFERENT and EXCITING! We're going to step back from heavy coding and explore something AMAZING: Artificial Intelligence, or AI! You've probably heard about AI—maybe ChatGPT, Siri, YouTube recommendations. But what IS AI really? Where do you see it? How does it work? That's what we're going to discover this term!"

**Set Expectations:**

> "This term is a 'breather'—we'll do some coding review to keep your skills fresh, but mostly we'll explore the world of AI. By the end, you'll understand how everything you've learned connects to the AI that helps you every day!"

#### Teaching Tips

- Be enthusiastic—AI is exciting!
- Address any fears early ("AI isn't scary, it's helpful!")
- Emphasize that they're already learning AI skills
- Make it feel like an adventure, not a break

### Part 2: What IS AI? (10 minutes)

#### Goals

- Define AI in age-appropriate terms
- Use concrete examples students know
- Make it simple and understandable

#### The Definition

**Write on board:**

**AI = Computers doing smart things that usually need human intelligence**

> "That's a big definition! Let me break it down with examples you know..."

#### Live Examples

**Example 1: Siri/Alexa**

> "When you talk to Siri, what happens?"

Wait for: It understands me, answers questions, etc.

> "Right! Siri UNDERSTANDS what you're saying. That's AI—understanding speech is something that needs intelligence!"

**Example 2: YouTube Recommendations**

> "When you open YouTube, what do you see?"

Wait for: Videos it thinks I'll like

> "Exactly! YouTube LEARNED what you like and suggests videos. That's AI—learning and making suggestions!"

**Example 3: Video Game Characters**

> "In video games, do the characters react to what you do?"

Wait for: Yes, they get smarter, adapt, etc.

> "Yes! Game characters that adapt and react are using AI!"

#### The Simple Answer

> "So AI is when computers can: understand, learn, make decisions, recognize patterns, and solve problems—things that usually need human intelligence!"

#### Teaching Tips

- Use examples students actually use
- Keep it simple—avoid technical jargon
- Ask questions to keep them engaged
- Use visual aids if possible (show Siri, YouTube, etc.)

---

### Part 3: AI Scavenger Hunt (10 minutes)

#### Goals

- Students actively identify AI in their lives
- Build awareness of how much AI they use
- Make it interactive and fun

#### The Activity

> "Now it's YOUR turn! Let's do an AI Scavenger Hunt! Work in pairs or small groups and make a list of AI you use or see every day!"

**Give them 5 minutes to brainstorm, then share:**

Go around the room and have groups share their lists. Write examples on the board.

**Common examples to expect:**
- Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant
- YouTube, Netflix, TikTok recommendations
- Video game characters
- Autocorrect
- Face recognition
- Photo organization
- Shopping suggestions
- Maps and navigation

**After sharing:**

> "Wow! Look at all this AI! You use AI ALL THE TIME! And you probably didn't even realize it was AI!"

#### Teaching Tips

- Encourage creativity—there are many examples
- Validate all reasonable answers
- Help groups that are stuck with gentle hints
- Celebrate when they find unexpected examples
- Use this to show how pervasive AI is

### Part 4: Regular vs AI Programs (10 minutes)

#### Goals

- Help students understand the difference
- Connect to what they've learned
- Show that AI builds on their skills

#### The Comparison

**Write on board:**

**Regular Programs (What You've Made):**
- Follow exact instructions
- Do the same thing every time
- Need you to tell them exactly what to do

**AI Programs:**
- Can learn from examples
- Can adapt and change
- Can recognize patterns
- Can make predictions

#### Code Example

**Show a program they've made:**

```python
score = 85
if score >= 80:
    print("You passed!")
else:
    print("Try again!")
```

> "This program ALWAYS does the same thing. If score is 80 or more, it says 'You passed!' That's a regular program."

**Now explain AI:**

> "But YouTube doesn't have a list like 'if user likes cats, show cat videos.' Instead, it WATCHES what you click and LEARNS what you like. Then it suggests new videos based on what it learned. That's AI—it learns and adapts!"

#### The Key Difference

> "Regular programs: You write exact rules. AI programs: They learn from examples. But here's the cool part: AI is built using the SAME skills you've learned—variables, math, if/else! You're learning the foundation!"

#### Teaching Tips

- Use code they've actually written
- Make the comparison clear
- Emphasize that AI builds on their skills
- Don't make regular programs seem "lesser"—they're both important!

### Part 5: Busting Myths (10 minutes)

#### Goals

- Address common fears about AI
- Make AI feel approachable, not scary
- Build confidence

#### Common Myths to Address

**Myth 1: "AI is Just Robots"**

> "Who thinks AI is just robots?"

Many hands might go up.

> "Actually, most AI isn't in robots! AI is in your phone, your computer, video games, apps, websites. Robots are just ONE place AI can live!"

**Myth 2: "AI Will Take Over the World"**

> "Have you heard that AI will take over? That's a myth! AI is a tool, like a hammer or calculator. It helps us do things, but it doesn't have feelings or want to take over. AI is created by humans to help humans!"

**Myth 3: "AI is Magic"**

> "AI might seem like magic, but it's not! It's math and programming—the same skills you've been learning! Variables, math, if/else, patterns. That's what powers AI!"

**Myth 4: "AI is Too Complicated for Kids"**

> "Actually, you're already learning AI skills! Every Python program you write uses concepts that power AI. You're building the foundation right now!"

#### Teaching Tips

- Be reassuring—address fears directly
- Use age-appropriate language
- Emphasize that AI helps us
- Make it feel accessible, not intimidating
- Encourage questions

---

### Part 6: Connecting to Python (10 minutes)

#### Goals

- Show how their skills connect to AI
- Build confidence that they're learning valuable skills
- Make them feel like they're already AI programmers

#### The Connection

**Go through each skill they've learned:**

**1. Print Statements → AI Output**

> "When Siri speaks, it's like a print() statement! When ChatGPT writes text, it's using output just like you!"

**2. Variables → AI Memory**

> "When Siri remembers your name, it's using variables! AI stores information just like `name = 'Alex'`!"

**3. Math → AI Calculations**

> "AI does MILLIONS of math calculations per second! Every recommendation, every decision uses math—just like you've been learning!"

**4. If/Else → AI Decisions**

> "AI makes decisions using if/else logic! 'If this pattern, then do that'—that's how AI thinks!"

**5. Elif → AI Multiple Choices**

> "AI often has many options to choose from, just like you learned with elif!"

#### The Big Picture

> "Everything you've learned is used in AI! You're not just learning Python—you're learning the foundation of AI! Every skill you've mastered is a skill that powers AI!"

#### Teaching Tips

- Be enthusiastic about the connections
- Use specific examples from their lessons
- Make them feel accomplished
- Build excitement for future learning

## 🤖 AI Activity: AI Scavenger Hunt (5-10 minutes)

### What to Do

1. **Introduction** (1 min)
   > "You've learned what AI is. Now let's find it! AI is everywhere - let's see how many things we can find that use AI!"

2. **Scavenger Hunt Activity** (4-5 min)
   - Give students 5 minutes to look around (home or classroom)
   - Have them list things that use AI: phones, computers, apps, games, etc.
   - Share findings: "Who found the most AI?"
   - Discuss: "What surprised you? Where did you find AI you didn't expect?"

3. **Discussion** (2-3 min)
   - "How many things did you find that use AI?"
   - "Where was AI in places you didn't expect?"
   - "Why do you think AI is everywhere?"

4. **Connection** (1 min)
   > "AI is everywhere! By finding AI in your daily life, you're becoming AI-aware - understanding where AI lives and how it helps you. This is the first step to becoming an AI expert!"

### Discussion Questions

- "How many things did you find that use AI?"
- "Where was AI in places you didn't expect?"
- "Why do you think AI is everywhere?"

### Teaching Tips

- Make it fun and competitive - "Who can find the most AI?"
- Encourage creative thinking - "What about your toothbrush? Your watch?"
- If in classroom, allow students to look around the room
- If virtual, have them look around their home
- Emphasize AI awareness: "You're becoming AI-aware!"

### Alternative Activity (If Short on Time)

- Quick discussion: "Who can name 5 things that use AI?"
- Connect: "AI is everywhere - you just learned to see it!"
- Emphasize: "Being AI-aware is the first step to becoming an AI expert!"

## 🎯 Assessment & Differentiation

### Formative Assessment

**During class, observe:**

- [ ] Can student define AI in simple terms?
- [ ] Can student identify AI examples?
- [ ] Does student understand regular vs AI programs?
- [ ] Is student engaged and curious?
- [ ] Does student see connections to their Python skills?

### Homework Assessment: "My AI Journal"

| Criteria | Exceeds (3) | Meets (2) | Developing (1) |
|----------|-------------|-----------|----------------|
| **Number of examples** | 6+ AI examples | 5 examples | Fewer than 5 |
| **Understanding** | Clear explanation of why each is AI | Some explanation | Little/no explanation |
| **Code quality** | Well-formatted, creative | Functional code | Basic or errors |
| **Comments** | Helpful, clear comments | Some comments | No comments |
| **Creativity** | Highly creative presentation | Creative elements | Basic list |

### Differentiation Strategies

#### For Students Who Need More Support

- Provide a template with examples to choose from
- Pair with a peer "AI buddy"
- Reduce requirement to 3 examples
- Focus on familiar AI (Siri, YouTube, games)
- Use visual aids and examples
- Provide sentence starters for comments

#### For Advanced Students

- Challenge with less obvious AI examples
- Ask them to explain HOW the AI works
- Encourage research into one specific AI
- Have them help peers identify AI
- Challenge with "Is this AI?" scenarios
- Ask them to predict future AI uses

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls & Solutions

### Conceptual Issues

| Problem | Prevention | Solution |
|---------|------------|----------|
| Students think AI is magic | Emphasize it's programming/math | "AI uses the same skills you're learning!" |
| Fear of AI taking over | Address myths directly | "AI is a tool created by humans to help humans" |
| Can't identify AI examples | Provide many examples first | Give hints, use familiar examples |
| Confused about regular vs AI | Use clear comparisons | Show code examples side-by-side |
| Overwhelmed by concept | Keep it simple, use examples | Focus on familiar AI they use daily |

### Engagement Issues

| Problem | Signs | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Students bored (not enough coding) | Disengaged, asking when they'll code | "We'll code next week! Today is about understanding AI" |
| Students confused | Can't answer questions | Simplify, use more examples, check understanding |
| Students scared | Expressing fear about AI | Address myths, emphasize AI helps us |
