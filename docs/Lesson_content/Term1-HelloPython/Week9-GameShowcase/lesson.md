- [ ] Your code runs without errors

## 🎤 Part 2: Showcase Presentations (30 minutes)

### How the Showcase Works

1. **When It's Your Turn:**

   - Share your screen so everyone can see your game
   - Introduce yourself and your game
   - Run your code — let the class help fill in the blanks!
   - Share one thing you're proud of
   - Unmute and take a bow! 🎭

2. **When Others Present:**
   - Stay muted but turn your camera on to show support
   - Type silly words in the chat when asked!
   - Unmute to laugh at the stories!
   - Unmute to clap and cheer after each presentation

### Your Presentation Script

Use this template if you're not sure what to say:

> "Hi! My name is **[your name]**.
>
> I built a Mad Libs game about **[your theme]**!
>
> Let me show you how it works...
>
> _[Run your code — ask the audience for words!]_
>
> My favorite part of this project was **[something you enjoyed]**.
>
> One thing I learned this term was **[something you learned]**.
>
> Thanks for playing!"

### Presentation Tips

**DO:**

- ✅ Speak clearly into your microphone
- ✅ Make sure your screen share is showing your code
- ✅ Ask the audience to type silly words in the chat — get them involved!
- ✅ Smile! Your camera is on and you made something cool!
- ✅ Say thank you at the end

**DON'T WORRY IF:**

- ❌ You feel nervous (everyone does!)
- ❌ You make a small mistake (no big deal!)
- ❌ The story is extra silly (that's the point!)
- ❌ You forget what to say (just describe what you see)
- ❌ Someone else has a similar theme (that's fine!)

### Being a Great Audience Member

When your classmates present:

- 👂 **Listen** — Give them your full attention (no other tabs!)
- 💬 **Participate** — Type silly words in the chat when asked!
- 😄 **React** — Use emoji reactions or unmute to laugh!
- 👏 **Clap** — Unmute to cheer after each presentation
- 💬 **Compliment** — Drop a kind message in the chat!

> _BrightByte says: "The best audiences are the ones who make presenters feel supported. Be the audience YOU would want when you present! And type the silliest words you can think of in the chat!"_

### Cool Projects Coming Up

In Term 2, you might build:

- 🧮 **Your Own Calculator** — A program that does math for you!
- 💰 **Tip Calculator** — Figure out restaurant tips
- 🎯 **Math Quiz Game** — Test your friends' math skills!
- 📊 **Grade Calculator** — Compute averages and scores

### A Tiny Preview

Here's a taste of what `input()` can do:

```python
# This is coming in Term 2!
name = input("What's your name? ")
print("Hello, " + name + "! Welcome to Math Wizard!")

number = int(input("Pick a number: "))
print(f"{number} x {number} = {number * number}")
```

When you run this, the user can TYPE their name and a number, and the program responds! How cool is that?!
